About the Study

The Middlebury Digital Learning Lab is conducting research on how students interact with and learn from digital information accessed through QR codes. This study aims to understand the effectiveness of QR code-based learning compared to traditional methods and to develop better educational tools for students.

Your participation will help shape the future of digital learning at Middlebury and beyond!

What You'll Do

Participants will attend three sessions over a two-week period. Each session involves:

  • Learning new material through digital resources accessed via QR codes
  • Completing short assessments to measure information retention
  • Providing feedback on your learning experience

The total time commitment is approximately 2.5-3 hours spread across three sessions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I can't make it to one of the sessions?

We strongly encourage attending all three sessions. If you have a scheduling conflict, please contact us as soon as possible, and we'll try to accommodate you.

How will the bonus compensation be determined?

Performance bonuses are based on a combination of assessment scores, engagement metrics, and completion of optional learning activities. Full details will be provided during the first session.

Will I learn what the study results are?

Yes! We'll share aggregated findings with all participants after the study concludes.

Is my data confidential?

Yes. All data collected will be anonymized for analysis and protected according to IRB guidelines. Your personal information will not be shared with anyone outside the research team.
